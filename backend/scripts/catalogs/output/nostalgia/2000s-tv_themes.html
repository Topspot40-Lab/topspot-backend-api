2000S TV THEMES
42 tracks • 39 artists

TV themes are sonic logos—hooks that paint characters before dialogue begins. In the live‑to‑air 1950s they leaned brassy and bold; the 1960s minted spy cool and sci‑fi wonder; the 1970s favored memorable riffs and sing‑along choruses; the 1980s embraced synths, drum machines, and crossover pop; the 1990s balanced band‑led earworms with cinematic scores; the 2000s surfaced prestige textures and clever needle‑drops; the 2010s turned openings into cultural events again; and the 2020s gave composers marquee status in the streaming era. A great theme is shorthand for a world. For show‑by‑show flavor in each decade, see the decade‑genre write‑ups.
