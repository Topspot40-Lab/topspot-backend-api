2020S COUNTRY
44 tracks • 31 artists • 2018–2022

Country is storytelling you can hum—front‑porch wisdom set to steel, fiddle, and a pocket that walks steady. In the 1950s, honky‑tonk grit and Nashville polish defined its twin identities; the 1960s refined studio craft and radio‑friendly choruses; the 1970s split lanes between outlaw rebellion and soft‑focus crossover; the 1980s codified the modern radio sound; the 1990s turned arena‑sized narratives into mainstream spectacles; the 2000s and 2010s mixed pop textures and roots revivals; and the 2020s favor honest writing that travels from TikTok to tailgates. What persists is voice—plainspoken but poetic—and songs that feel like family stories. For how country flexes in each era and who carried the torch decade by decade, check the decade‑genre descriptions.
