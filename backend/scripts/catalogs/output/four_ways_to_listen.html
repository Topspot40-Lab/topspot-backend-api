Four Ways to Listen

TopSpot40 offers four distinct listening experiences, each designed to help listeners discover music in a different way.

1. Nostalgia Programs

Nostalgia Programs recreate the experience of classic countdown radio by organizing music by decade and genre. Each program contains ranked songs, narrated introductions, artist information, historical context, and Spotify-integrated music playback.

Examples include:

  • 1950s Country
  • 1960s Rock
  • 1970s Pop
  • 1980s Country
  • 1990s Latin Global
  • 2000s R&B Soul

2. Collections

Collections organize music around themes, traditions, cultures, historical topics, and shared experiences. Unlike Nostalgia Programs, Collections are not limited to a single decade or genre. Instead, they bring together songs connected by a common story.

Examples include:

  • Railroad & Train Songs
  • Traditional Hymns
  • Patriotic Favorites
  • Great American Songbook
  • Mexican-American Favorites
  • Cowboy Songs & Western Favorites

3. Artist Spotlight

Artist Spotlight focuses on individual artists. Featured Artists include narrated biographies, historical background, career highlights, and curated track selections designed to tell the story of an artist's life and music.

TopSpot40 includes more than 329 Featured Artists with narrated biographies available in English, Spanish, and Portuguese.

Examples include:

  • B.B. King
  • Johnny Cash
  • Selena
  • Willie Nelson
  • Loretta Lynn
  • Frank Sinatra

4. Radio Modes

Radio Modes combine narration, artist information, storytelling, rankings, and Spotify playback into a continuous listening experience. The goal is to recreate some of the magic of traditional radio while taking advantage of modern streaming technology.

TopSpot40 is not designed to help people listen to more music. It is designed to help people connect more deeply with the music they already love.
Spotify provides the licensed music streaming experience. TopSpot40 enhances the experience through rankings, artist biographies, storytelling, music history, and music discovery.
